What we humans “see” isn’t like a raw image captured by a camera, but input heavily processed by our brain, merging images from both eyes, filling in details, and making lots of guesses. Our perception is generated by our brains, and not the literal light waves hitting our retinas. Therefore, what people “see” is subject to their beliefs, and what they have been taught. This also allows for change, but no technology can do that for you. Many people, especially white people, can’t see the small microaggressions that they commit in their daily lives, and a pair of new eyes won't change that, as the real culprit is the brain.
